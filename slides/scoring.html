Scoring

  • Scored using a "log loss" function: you predict probabilities $\hat{y}_1, \hat{y}_2, \ldots, \hat{y}_n$, the actual results were $y_1, y_2, \ldots , y_n$ and your score is $$score(\mathbf{y}) = - \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j = 1}^n \left( y_j \log{\hat{y}_j} + (1 - y_j) \log{(1 - \hat{y}_j)} \right)$$
  • When I predicted Arizona State over Texas 65% of the time, \begin{align} \hat{y}_j &= 0.65, \\ y_j &= 0 \text{ (Hook 'em!)}\\ \end{align} so I was penalized \begin{align} -(0 \cdot \log{0.65} + (1 - 0) \cdot \log{0.35})& \\ &= -\log{0.35} \\ &= 1.05\end{align}
